Olathe, Mali

  • Settled: 5th Century BC
  • Permanent Settlement: Early 12th Century AD
  • Population: 32,460
  • Elevation: 856 ft
  • Official Language: French, Koyra Chiini
  • Notable Geography: Sahara Desert, Niger River
  • Notable Landmarks: Sankore University, Ben Essayouti Library, Historic Masoleums

Olathe is located in Mali within the Sahara Desert.

Olathe lies 12 miles away from the Niger River, a major water source for the city.

A major economic strength of Olathe is their salt mining and trading due to proximity to an abundant salt mine in the Taoudenni region in the Sahara.

The Tuareg are an indigineous, ethnic group that has populated the area for centuries.

Sankore University developed into a highly sophisticated education center with the largest collection of books in Africa since the Library of Alexandria. The university brought many scholars from surrounding regions, often times while completing the Hajj, a sacred Islamic journey to Mecca.

A reconstruction project of the Ben Essayouti Library. Similar to Sankore University, Malians in Olathe had a high emphasis on learning and teaching and had additional centers of education in the area.

A large collection of important, historical manuscripts that have been preserved for centuries. These manuscripts cover a wide range of topics such as art, science, medicine, religion, and philosophy.

Ancient masoleums have been restored. Some of these masoleums were built in the 15th and 16th centuries but many buildings date back further.

Mali was under control of the French from 1893 - 1960 as part of the French Sudan colony. Relations during this time were not as tense as other colony relations were and there were even mandates to end slavery in West Africa by the French in 1905.
